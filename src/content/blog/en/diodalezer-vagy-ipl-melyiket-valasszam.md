Two technologies dominate the permanent hair removal market: **diode laser** and **IPL (Intense Pulsed Light)**. But which is the better choice if you want to open or grow a salon? In this article, we compare the two technologies in detail.

## What's the difference?

### Diode laser

The diode laser operates on a **single, precise wavelength** (typically 755nm, 808nm, 940nm or 1064nm). This concentrated light acts directly on the melanin in the hair, effectively destroying the hair follicle.

**Advantages:**
- Greater effectiveness
- Faster treatments
- Works on darker skin too (1064nm)
- Fewer treatments required
- More precise, more targeted effect

### IPL

IPL uses **broad-spectrum light** (500-1200nm). The various wavelengths act simultaneously, but this reduces effectiveness compared to laser-based solutions.

**Advantages:**
- Cheaper device
- Versatile (can also be used for pigmentation)
- Simpler treatment

## Effectiveness comparison

| Aspect | Diode laser | IPL |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| Number of treatments | 6-8 | 10-12 |
| Durability of results | 80-90% | 60-70% |
| Pain-free comfort | Better (with cooling) | Moderate |
| Treating dark skin | Yes (1064nm) | Limited |

## Return on investment (ROI)

Although the diode laser device is more expensive, it delivers a **faster return on investment**:

- **Higher revenue from fewer treatments** - higher treatment prices
- **More satisfied clients** - better results = more referrals
- **Faster treatments** - more clients per day

### Example calculation

The price of an average diode laser hair removal session: **15,000-25,000 Ft / session**

If you perform just 4 treatments per day:
- Daily revenue: ~80,000 Ft
- Monthly revenue: ~1,600,000 Ft
- **The device pays for itself within 1-2 months!**
